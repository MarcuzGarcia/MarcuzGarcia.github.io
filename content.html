Important Lives

We all have been, or at least know someone who became severely ill or sick. Additionally, we would always feel so bad that these people have to go through such pain. Sometimes we let them get treated at the doctor or the hospital. Some get better and recover, and some people’s health worsens. But, nothing could change the fact that money and resources were used up by healthcare to try to help. So, it is better to end the suffering of those that have serious and/or contagious diseases and those that are near death’s door than for the healthcare system to waste time and assets on these people.

For those that have severe or uncurable disorders, since they are helpless and only give the healthcare system more problems to solve because they have a very slim chance of surviving, they should just be left to die. A lot of funds are used to try to cure or relieve the disease that person has. Moreover, only 30 percent of health professionals address the health needs of most people. That’s why we should prioritize and cure those that have only mild conditions. When a very ill person dies while health workers were treating them, then the efforts of the medical workers are rendered useless. And, when a person is cured and survives, was it worth all that money to help that one patient? Would you get an ambulance or a surgery for someone that is on the verge of dying? They should be thankful that they get to die a peaceful death rather than continue to struggle for survival. It is only just a matter of time and a test of patience until they finally pass away. When these people have passed away, their bodies can still be of use to us. We can use their bodies to contribute more to the study on the human body and its functions.

Now, for the people that have contagious or infectious illnesses, they should receive the same treatment as those that were aforementioned. These people would not only endanger well-being of the people around them, but also healthcare workers who try to help them. The number of healthcare workers in the Philippines is already low, but if they would become infected too, then the number of employees here would decrease even more than before. And, if these workers decrease, then more money will be used to try to help them, and the painful cycle repeats. In our situation with COVID-19 in the Philippines, we can also see this issue. Confirmed cases consist of around 0.32% of our population, which may not seem a lot, but these people who are infected might spread it to healthy people. We need to prioritize the lives of those that are confirmed to be free of these infectious diseases and leave those that are sick to die a slow death. So, this will help the healthcare system make better use of its funds and not sacrifice any of our precious healthcare workers and our healthy citizens.

Lastly, for old people or the elderly, they are more at risk of getting sick. They are prone to many illnesses and are more likely to suffer the most when they get sick. Additionally, we all are going to die whether we like it or not; the elderly usually go sooner than us. So, we should end it right there so that the healthcare system does not have to deal with a person who is nearing the end of their lives. They should not have to waste time and money to assist someone helpless. The risk of old people, in their 60s and 70s, getting infected, especially in the time of a pandemic, is relatively very high, and having to help all of them would not only ruin the state of the healthcare’s assets but also give people false hope that we can cure all of them. In conclusion, it is like beating a dead horse, the fact that they will die soon will only be a waste of the healthcare’s budget. To add to that, we should prioritize the young since they still have many years to come.

In conclusion, those that are at death’s door and/or those that impose danger to the health of people have no hope at all. And, we should not use any of our money on them because, in the end, all of our blood, sweat, and tears to help them will be for nothing. We would just be wasting The government needs to understand what they should spend the money they have to better this country. The same goes for the Philippines' healthcare – they should know which patients are to be prioritized.
